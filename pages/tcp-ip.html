Internet Term 2

TCP/IP

This page explains TCP/IP using its definition, role, and analogy.

Definition

TCP/IP is a suite of communication protocols used to connect devices over the internet. TCP ensures reliable delivery of data, while IP handles addressing and routing.

Role

It enables data transmission between devices by breaking it into packets, sending them, and reassembling them correctly.

Analogy

Like sending a parcel in pieces—each piece travels separately but is reassembled at the destination.
